Physical Improvement

Prepare for the gains!

I assume you are either a beginner or intemediate athlete

I am going to omit technical details and present information, I wish I had when I started my fitness journey. Most critical This is Not medical advice what follows are my personal opinions. Based on personal experience.



  • Step 1(Basic information):

    Understanding that You can start right now. Going to a gym is not essential to Physical Improvement. A plan is required regardless of whether you choose to go to the gym or workout at home.


  • Step 2(Basic Nutrition):

    Calculate Base calories
    In the pursuit of muscle gain(BULKING): Base calories + 250-750 calories Training intensity covered in next list item
    In the pursuit of fat loss(CUTTING): Base calories - 250-750 calories Training intensity covered in next list item
    Start at 250 calories for both Bulking and Cutting and only adjust if progress has stagnated.

    Calulate cals per food item
    Macros vs Micros

    Macros
    Carbs, Fats, Protein
    1g Carbs = 4 calories
    1g Protein = 4 calories
    1g Fat = 9 calories

    Micro's
    Micronutrients are vitamins and minerals needed by the body in very small amounts. However, their impact on a body's health are critical, and deficiency in any of them can cause severe and even life-threatening conditions. 100% did not just google this. ;)

    Food Selection choice, simple guide for beginners.
    70% healthy/30% cheat meals.

    Food Selection choice, simple guide for Intemediates.
    90% healthy/10% cheat meals. At this point you have sufficent training knowledge to adapt your nutrition if need be.

    Healthy does not imply Flavourless


  • Step 3(Training Intensity):

    Training intensity refers to how close you are to training to failure!

    Example: Let's say your max squat is 100kg for 1 rep. You then squat 100kg for 1 rep, You have trained to failure at this point, Reps in Reserve(RIR) 0, Rate of Perceived Excursion(RPE 10) Read More
    As a beginner judging your own intensity will be hard, so your first training cycle(4-6 weeks) should be used more as a learning opportunity to enhance future training.

    As a beginner your training intensity will be quite low during the first few weeks of training! This low intensity is sufficient for making progress(Gains), do not rush the process.
    Majority of your GAINS will be made during your first year of lifting, Assuming you train with intent...Take it seriously!

    As an intemediate your training intensity will be always be moderate, raising to max quite often! Is this nessecasy? No, but if fatigue is managed all will be good.
    (4-12) week training cycles. Deloads included(Fatigue management in next list item)

    Methods of progression!


  • Step 4(Fatigue management)

    High intensity + volume = higher recovery time. Always training to failure is not a good idea. Unless sport/training specific.

    Fatigue management strategies:
    Sleep, A Great 7 - 8 hrs hopefully REM cycle kicks in. Pray to the Youtube Fitness Gods.
    Healthy food intake(recovery may be better at higher food consumption and vice versa)
    Deload weeks(Purposely lowering training variables for a week or 2 weeks to lower fatigue and prepare you for later training.)
    Ice baths, Saunas, Massages (Not directly improve recovery but may have a PLACEBO effect.)
    Special exercises(Shoulder breakers, Face pulls, Band-pull-aparts, Band work) at low intensity.
    Streching(Static at times outside training, Dynamic during,before and/or after training)


  • Step 5 (Specificity of training)

    I would rank this higher then step 4 but as a beginner we tend to train a lot harder then we need to


    TO get better at something you should devote more time and resources toward that thing


    Specificity of training, Planning your training according to a specfic goal. Eg: Bodybuilding/Powerlifting, There will be overlap with various sports and training. Do not allow this overlap to completely take over your curent training.
    If you plan a program for hypertrophy(Bodybuilding) but include 30 minutes of hard cardio at the beginning of each workout you are violating specificity and orienting your training more to that of an endurance athlete. DO NOT BE DUMB.

    Specificity explanied


  • Step 6 (Committment to training)

    Rome was not built in a day
    Change takes time.

    How long should your training be?

    Making each week harder (Accumulation)

    Accumulation = Training that starts with low volume / intensity and the gradual increase of training variables over the weeks of training.

    Dropping fatigue (Deload)

    Deload = Period of Low training intensity and volume lasting a week to several weeks if need be

    Accumulation + Deload = Mesocycle

    Mesocycles of the same type of training = Training Block

    Length of Mesocycle, this can be anywhere from 4 - 6 weeks with 1 week deload. I would suggest a beginner to run a 4 week meso with 1 week of deload, Total 5 weeks of training. Repeat this for 2 - 4 Mesos.

    Then take a longer deload if need be and return to training or change. Eg: Bodybuilding to Powerlifting Build a muscular base then get stronger

    If your program is 12 weeks long, run it DO not quit without very good reason
    Bitching out is not good reason
